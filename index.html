Welcome to A$AP FTC

We are a FIRST Tech Challenge team based in South Jordan, Utah. We are a group of high school students who are passionate about STEM and robotics.

We are committed to proving ourselves as a rookie team by developing consistent robotics systems.

Founded in 2024, we are a community-based team that strives to rethink the way we approach robotics and STEM education. We believe that robotics is not just about building robots, but also about learning and growing as individuals.

A$AP is committed to accelerating progress and achieving success with agility and precision.

Our mission is to foster a culture of rapid innovation and relentless determination in the field of robotics.

We aim to inspire and be inspired, to challenge and be challenged, and to continuously strive for excellence.

United in our efforts, we aim to push the boundaries of what’s possible, transforming ideas into reality and daring to venture into the extraordinary.

For more information about the team, please visit our About Us page.

About FIRST

FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a global nonprofit organization that aims to inspire young people to be science and technology leaders and innovators. Through engaging, mentor-based programs, FIRST builds science, engineering, and technology skills, inspires innovation, and fosters well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, communication, and leadership.

Founded in 1989 by inventor Dean Kamen, FIRST provides opportunities for students of all ages to participate in hands-on robotics competitions and STEM learning experiences.

Learn more about FIRST on their official website.

About FTC

FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) is one of the programs offered by FIRST. It is designed for students in grades 7-12 to compete head-to-head using a sports model. Teams are responsible for designing, building, and programming their robots to compete in an alliance format against other teams.

FTC emphasizes Gracious Professionalism®, teamwork, and innovation. Participants learn valuable skills such as problem-solving, collaboration, and project management, while also gaining hands-on experience in engineering and programming.

Each season, teams are presented with a new challenge that encourages creativity and critical thinking. The program not only prepares students for careers in STEM but also helps them develop skills that are essential for success in any field.

Learn more about FTC on their official page.
